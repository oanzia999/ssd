Understanding Stroke Prevention

According to the NHS, a stroke is a serious life-threatening medical condition that happens when the blood supply to part of the brain is cut off.

The F.A.S.T. Test

  • Face: Has their face fallen on one side? Can they smile?
  • Arms: Can they lift both arms and keep them there?
  • Speech: Is their speech slurred?
  • Time: Time to call 999 immediately if you see any of these signs.
Medical Curiosity:

Did you know that during a stroke, the brain can lose up to 1.9 million brain cells per minute? This highlights why rapid prediction and treatment are critical.

Risk Factors We Monitor

Our application tracks key indicators identified by the NHS, including high blood pressure (hypertension), which is the biggest single risk factor for stroke. Other factors include diabetes, high cholesterol, and atrial fibrillation.
